01 / The Product
Three models.
Every edge case.
BADAS 2.0 is a family of collision prediction models built on VJEPA-2 attention-based architecture. From cloud to IoT, every deployment scenario is covered. 5x the training data of BADAS 1.5.
BADAS 2.0
300M params / Teacher
94% mAP. State-of-the-art collision prediction. Attention-based visual explainability. Text descriptions. Voice capability. The full model.
BADAS 2.0 Flash
87M params / Real-time
Edge-optimized. Real-time inference on GPU/CPU. Rivals BADAS 1.5 performance at a fraction of compute.
BADAS 2.0 Flashlight
22M params / Ultra-Edge
IoT-ready. False alarm prevention specialist. Better than BADAS 1.0 at 1/14th the parameters.
New Capabilities
Visual explainability via attention maps. Natural language descriptions of predicted events. Voice interface for fleet operators. These are not incremental features, they are category-defining differentiators.

03 / The Narrative
Open challenge positioning.
We publish. We invite comparison.
The launch narrative is built on radical transparency. Publish benchmarks publicly. Challenge competitors to match them on the same data. Yann LeCun amplification validates his world model thesis.
Pillar 01
Open Challenge
Publish benchmarks. Invite direct comparison. Transparency as competitive advantage. No one else dares to share their data.
Pillar 02
Yann LeCun Amplification
VJEPA-2 architecture validates his world model thesis. This is the proof that attention-based models beat autoregressive for safety-critical applications.
Pillar 03
Explainability Story
Attention maps show exactly what the model sees. Natural language explains why it predicted a collision. Fleet operators get voice interface. Insurance gets audit trail.
Pillar 04
Edge-to-Cloud Family
One model family from 22M params (IoT dashcam) to 300M (cloud analytics). No competitor covers this range. Every buyer finds their deployment fit.
Target Audience
Fleet operators, insurance underwriters, OEM safety engineers, AV/ADAS teams, government DOTs. The explainability story lands differently for each: operators want voice alerts, insurers want audit trails, OEMs want integration specs.
